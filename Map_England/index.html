Housing prices of England and Wales

Housing prices across England and Wales for the period 2017

Map of England and Wales

This first map shows a map of England and Wales for the period 2017 including major known cities around the country. You can sort between different maps to show the overall average across the country or averages for specific types of housing such as Detached or Semi-Detached.

Detached housing is considered to be a standalone house while Semi-Detached is considered to be a house that has two seperate homes within the housing seperated by a wall. Terraced housing is considered to be mulptiple homes connected together in a row, while flats are self contained homes found within complexes. Other includes any other type of home that does not fall in any of these categories

Map of London

London is the most expensive place found within the UK with average housing prices for some property types ranging up to 15,000,000 pounds. A new scale is need to properly show the range of average housing prices as places in the heart of the mega city do not have an average price less 1,000,000 pound while locations on the outside of the city have housing as cheap as 290,000 pounds.

One thing to note about this map is that it does not consider the size of the housing purchased so while some hosuing may be cheaper than others it is likely that the size of the housing could be considerably smaller to the more expsensive property type.

It also understandable why some housing is considerably more expensive to other housing as Detached home in the City of London would be a rare sight where flats in the City of London would be easy to come by.

Map of Wales and the Northwest

Wales and the Northwest of the country are interesting places to look at as most of wales is farmland and comparatively cheap compared to rest country with housing cheaper than 100,000 pounds and with housing only as expensive as 1,500,000 pounds which is possibly explained by the population of wales not being as large as the rest of the country.

The Northwest is interesting as it containes some of the UK's largest cities; Manchester and Liverpool which you think would priced quite highly however hosuing as cheap as 100,000 to 150,000 can be found in both which pales in comparison to prices in London but this is likely due there being less demand in these cities comapred to London due to how 'rough' they're perceived.

Again in both cases the housing classification of Other is considerably much higher than the other categories as likely contains housing that involves a large amount of land or a whole building that does not fall within the other definitions
